Thou blind fool, Love, what dost thou to mine eyes
That they behold and see not what they see?
They know what beauty is, see where it lies,
What gold in hue it holds and what substance it gives;
If thou behold their heart, it knows thee
The deceiver whose worth is in their heart.
For their minds in thee, they hear nothing but lies,
And thou to themselves drowns this deaf ear.
O, let them hear how I tell the truth,
Which thou must hear from thee; for when I am in sleep
They hear not that thy deeds lie in them,
Nor know thou thy beauty to be false.
  But that thy heart may hear thy truth thou wilt know
  Because it hath told it so.
