In faith, I do not love thee with mine eyes,
For they in thee a thousand errors note;
But 'tis my heart that loves what they despise,
And sees a virtue in what seems dull.
I love thee not for how a pleasing look
May change the soul that looks on thy face,
But 'neath the surface for beauty's sake
A constant soul, true, true, and living.
So let my eyes their outward grace spy,
That if thy features thou be, there is no other way,
Thy truth is my only heaven and my earth,
My true love for a perfection unseen.
   Let me love for a reason what is born of love,
   And in what I love, let me live for eternity.

Lo, as a careful huswife runs to catch
One of her feathered creatures broke away,
Sets down her babe, and makes all swift dispatch,
The chase is put down; but now her love,
To what it fled, finds no dwelling place
But in my thoughts, to love thee anew.
For what the world loves and how the tongue
Doth rehearse its love are all a matter
Of some secret love, for no man can tell
The heart's secret passion is in my eyes,
And robs the world of true joy, for my love is found.
So must I love thee, and keep thee alive,
For I know not how to give away
My sole life to a single, self-same fate.
