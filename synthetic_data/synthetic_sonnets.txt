###1###
When Phoebus doth his golden chariot guide,
And banishes the shadows of the night,
My waking thoughts to thee do swiftly ride,
And fill my soul with uncorrupted light.
But when the sun descends and day doth wane,
And sable clouds do cloak the heavens' face,
My joys do flee, my spirit feels the pain,
And absence steals from me all sweet embrace.
Yet in my dreams, thy beauty doth appear,
A phantom fair, that mocks my waking woe,
Dispelling doubt and banishing all fear,
Until the morning bids the vision go.
   So in my sleep, I find a sweet relief,
   But wake to find my pleasure turned to grief.

###2###
Thy voice, a lute that sweetly doth resound,
Doth capture all the senses of my ear,
And where thy gentle footsteps do abound,
All worldly cares do vanish and grow clear.
Thy eyes, like stars that twinkle in the dark,
Do guide my soul through tempests and through strife,
And on my heart they leave a burning mark,
Infusing it with vibrant, living life.
Thy smile, a dawn that breaks upon the morn,
Doth chase away the shadows of despair,
And from my soul, all weary grief is torn,
And leaves me buoyant, free from every care.
   So art thou all that nature can devise,
   A perfect form, reflected in mine eyes.

###3###
The passing years, like thieves in silent tread,
Do steal away the bloom from beauty's cheek,
And leave behind a visage pale and dread,
Where once a youthful radiance did speak.
The strength of youth, that once did know no bound,
Doth fade and falter, weakened by the fray,
And furrowed lines on brow and chin are found,
Where laughter's mirth did hold its joyful sway.
But in thy soul, a beauty doth reside,
That time itself can never hope to spoil,
A gentle grace, that ever shall abide,
And blossom forth, despite the earthly toil.
   So let the world grow old and turn to dust,
   Thy spirit's grace shall ever be my trust.

###4###
When winter's frost doth grip the barren land,
And all the trees are stripped of leafy pride,
I think of thee, with summer in my hand,
And in thy warmth, my frozen heart doth hide.
The biting wind that whistles through the air,
Recalls the chill that absence doth impart,
But then thy memory, beyond compare,
Doth bring a sudden thaw unto my heart.
The birds are hushed, no joyful song they raise,
As if the world itself had lost its glee,
But in my mind, I sing thy endless praise,
And find a spring that blossoms just for me.
   So though the world be clad in hues of grey,
   Thy love, my sun, shall light my darkest day.

###5###
Doth Fortune smile, or frown with bitter rage,
It matters not, so long as thou art near,
Thou art the muse that turns my ink to sage,
And dries the falling of a foolish tear.
When adverse winds do buffet and do blow,
And tempests dark do gather in the sky,
Thy steadfast love a steady beacon show,
And bid my fearful, trembling spirit fly.
The world may judge with harsh and cruel decree,
And cast its stones with malice in its eye,
But thy pure heart doth ever set me free,
And makes my worldly troubles pass me by.
   So let them rage, let fortune take its course,
   With thee beside, I find my greatest force.

###6###
The fleeting moments, like swift streams that flow,
Do bear us onward to the distant sea,
And leave behind the seeds that we do sow,
For future years to reap eternally.
The laughter shared, the tears that we have shed,
The whispered vows that fell upon the air,
Are woven in the tapestry we've spread,
A chronicle of burdens we did bear.
So let us cherish every precious hour,
And in each kiss, a deeper meaning find,
For in our love, there lies a potent power,
That leaves the fleeting world and time behind.
   Thus shall our bond, through all the ages last,
   A love that time itself cannot outcast.

###7###
When envious tongues do wag and spread their lies,
And whisper slanders that do wound and sting,
I turn my gaze unto thy honest eyes,
And find the solace that thy truth doth bring.
The serpent's hiss, the viper's venomous dart,
May seek to poison innocence and good,
But thy pure spirit, from the very start,
Hath ever been misunderstood.
For in thy breast, no malice can be found,
But only kindness, gentle and profound,
And where thy faithful, loving steps are bound,
True virtue's everlasting song is crowned.
   So let them speak, their poisoned words are vain,
   Thy noble soul shall evermore remain.

###8###
The silent stars, that keep their lonely watch,
Do seem to mock the passions of the day,
As if our human, fleeting joys did botch,
The grand design that heavens do display.
The moon, a pearl upon the velvet night,
Doth shed a pallor on the sleeping earth,
And in its beams, I see thy spectral light,
A ghost of beauty, of celestial birth.
But though thou art removed from mortal sight,
And dwell'st afar, beyond my earthly ken,
Thy presence fills my soul with pure delight,
And lifts me up, again and yet again.
   So though the stars may gleam with distant fire,
   Thy love doth feed my soul's unquenched desire.

###9###
My quill, though dipped in ink of darkest hue,
Doth find its purest inspiration's spring,
When thoughts of thee, so constant and so true,
Do lend my humble muse her soaring wing.
The verses penned, that seem to flow with ease,
Are but the echoes of thy spoken word,
The gentle breeze that whispers through the trees,
A melody that I have deeply heard.
And though the rhymes may falter and may stray,
And metaphors may trip upon the tongue,
If they but hint of thee, in any way,
Then shall my praise be ever truly sung.
   So let my words, however weak they be,
   Bear witness to my love, O, none but thee.

###10###
The roseate blush that mantles thy fair face,
Doth shame the dawn and all its rosy hue,
And in thy smile, I find a heaven's grace,
A beauty that is ever fresh and new.
The silken strands that crown thy noble head,
Are spun from sunlight, woven by the breeze,
And where thy gentle footsteps have been led,
All nature seems to bow upon its knees.
Thy voice, a strain from some celestial choir,
Doth charm the savage breast and tame the wild,
And fills my soul with passion and with fire,
A love by earthly limits undefiled.
   So art thou fair, beyond all mortal dream,
   A perfect being, of love's purest gleam.

###11###
When melancholy's shadow doth descend,
And heavy thoughts do weigh upon my mind,
I seek the comfort that thy words can lend,
And leave the lingering shades of grief behind.
For in thy presence, troubles seem to fade,
And anxious cares do loosen their strong hold,
Thy gentle spirit, by no fault betrayed,
Is worth to me far more than purest gold.
The world may boast of riches and of fame,
But these are trifles, fleeting and untrue,
Compared to that which bears thy cherished name,
My dearest love, my solace, and my you.
   So let the world its transient glories chase,
   I find my treasure in thy kind embrace.

###12###
The fleeting joys that mortals do pursue,
Are but as shadows, cast by passing light,
And in the end, they vanish from our view,
And leave us lonely in the endless night.
The laughter bright, the merriment so keen,
Doth fade and dwindle, leaving naught behind,
But empty echoes of what once had been,
A bitter sorrow for the heart to find.
But in thy love, a constant flame doth burn,
A fire that time itself cannot destroy,
To thee, my faithful heart shall ever turn,
And in thy gaze, I find my lasting joy.
   So let the world its fleeting pleasures seek,
   My truest happiness, within thee, I speak.

###13###
When Fortune's wheel doth turn with cruel intent,
And casts me down from heights of sweet content,
Thy constant faith, on which my soul is bent,
Doth shield me from the blows that have been sent.
The storms of life may rage with furious might,
And threaten to engulf me in their sway,
But in thy love, I find a guiding light,
That leads me safe unto a brighter day.
The envious gaze, the whisper of despair,
May seek to dim the luster of our bond,
But our true hearts, beyond all earthly care,
Shall stand united, firm and ever fond.
   So let them rage, let tempests loudly roar,
   Our love shall triumph, now and evermore.

###14###
Thou art the balm that soothes my wounded soul,
The gentle hand that wipes away my tears,
Thou art the star that makes my spirit whole,
And banishes my most consuming fears.
The world may offer treasures, rich and rare,
But none can match the value of thy grace,
Thy precious love, beyond all compare,
Doth make my heart a more abiding place.
So let me pledge, with all my heart and might,
My steadfast faith, my service, and my all,
To thee, my love, my guiding, purest light,
Until the day when shadows do befall.
   For in thy love, I find my true release,
   And in thy arms, I find eternal peace.

###15###
The cruel decree that bids us be apart,
Doth pierce my soul with agony untold,
And leaves a void within my lonely heart,
A story that can never be made whole.
The miles that stretch between us, vast and deep,
Are like a gulf that separates the shore,
And while the world in peaceful slumber sleep,
My sorrowing spirit doth forever soar.
Yet in my dreams, I hold thee close and near,
And in thy phantom embrace, I find delight,
Dispelling every doubt and every fear,
Until the dawning of the morning light.
   So though the day doth bring me naught but pain,
   My sleeping hours with thee, I live again.

###16###
When golden hues of autumn fill the wood,
And leaves like coins do tumble from the trees,
I think of all the love we understood,
And feel the whisper of a gentle breeze.
The summer's warmth, though now it starts to fade,
Doth leave behind a lingering embrace,
A memory of joys that we have made,
Reflected in the smile upon thy face.
The world grows old, and beauty starts to wane,
As nature yields unto the winter's sway,
But in our hearts, a love shall still remain,
That shall not fade with passing of the day.
   So let the seasons turn and time take flight,
   Our love shall burn with ever-constant light.

###17###
The fleeting touch that lingers on my skin,
The whispered word that trembles on thy breath,
Doth draw me in, where happiness doth begin,
And banish thoughts of sorrow and of death.
The stolen glance, the smile that doth impart,
A world of passion, held within its gleam,
Doth captivate and steal away my heart,
And make my waking moments like a dream.
The world may frown, and deem our love too bold,
But what know they of depths that we do share?
A story in our hearts that shall unfold,
Beyond the reach of worldly doubt or care.
   So let them judge, their wisdom is but slight,
   Our love shall blossom in the darkest night.

###18###
When envious eyes do scrutinize my deed,
And whisper tales that slander and defame,
I find in thee a solace for my need,
A shield against the venom of their blame.
Thy steadfast faith, a fortress strong and true,
Doth stand against the onslaught of their hate,
And in thy gaze, my spirit finds anew,
The strength to overcome a bitter fate.
For though the world may cast its cruel decree,
And seek to tarnish what is pure and good,
My heart knows well, where loyalty doth be,
And in thy truth, my faithful soul hath stood.
   So let them rage, their malice shall not sway,
   My love for thee, it shall not fade away.

###19###
The painted pomp that worldly kings do wear,
Doth hold no charm, no luster for mine eye,
Compared to that which thou dost justly bear,
A beauty that doth far surpass the sky.
The gems they hoard, the treasures they possess,
Are fleeting baubles, destined to decay,
But in thy soul, true riches do possess,
A worth that shall not wither or give way.
The outward show, that captivates the crowd,
Is but a mask, a hollow, empty guise,
But thy pure heart, both honest and unbowed,
Doth hold the truth that opens all our eyes.
   So let them boast of riches and of might,
   Thy gentle spirit is my soul's true light.

###20###
When all the world seems cloaked in deepest night,
And shadows long do stretch across the land,
Thy memory shines, a beacon ever bright,
And guides me onward with thy loving hand.
The icy grip of winter's chilling breeze,
Doth stir no fear within my steadfast breast,
For in my heart, thy warmth doth ever please,
And grants my weary spirit perfect rest.
The fading stars, that once did brightly gleam,
May lose their luster as the night grows late,
But thy dear love, a constant, living stream,
Doth conquer time and override all fate.
   So though the world may fade and turn to dust,
   My love for thee shall ever be my trust.
   
###21###
When that the sun doth hide his golden face,
And shadows long do stretch across the land,
My heart doth sink into a lonely place,
Where naught but sorrow can my soul command.
The birds no more their cheerful carols sing,
But all is hush'd, and silence holds her sway,
And bitter thoughts their mournful tidings bring,
To darken still the remnant of the day.
My dearest love, where art thou fled from me?
Thou art the light that banish'd all my gloom,
Without thy smile, no joy can e'er I see,
But dwell I must within this living tomb.
O come again, and banish all my fear,
And make my clouded, weary spirit clear.

###22###
The bloom of youth, a fleeting, fragile thing,
Doth wither fast, though beauty may abound,
And silver hairs upon the temples spring,
Where raven locks did once in glory crown'd.
The strength of limbs, that leap'd with youthful zest,
Doth yield at last to time's relentless hand,
And weary bones do crave a gentle rest,
As life's bright sun sinks low on distant strand.
Yet in the soul, a fire may still remain,
A wisdom bought with years of toil and strife,
A deeper love, that conquers worldly pain,
And sees the worth of every precious life.
So let us prize the moments as they fly,
For time, alas, will steal them by and by.

###23###
My muse, when thou dost bid my quill to write,
Doth hesitate, and feeleth much dismay,
To paint with words a beauty shining bright,
That doth outshine the dawning of the day.
Thy form is such, it doth all praise transcend,
Thy voice a music, sweet beyond compare,
Upon thy grace my fervent thoughts do bend,
And weave a tapestry beyond all care.
No mortal hand, nor painter's skillful eye,
Can e'er portray the essence of thy worth,
Thou art a star that shines in heavens high,
A living marvel, born of earthly birth.
So I must fail, though ardour doth inflame,
To capture fully thy celestial name.

###24###
The rose doth blush, and courts the morning dew,
Its tender petals, soft as velvet's touch,
A fleeting beauty, radiant and new,
That summer's breeze doth promise overmuch.
But soon the sun, with fiercer, burning ray,
Doth steal its sweetness, and its colour fade,
And winds unkind do bear its bloom away,
To lie forgotten in the verdant glade.
So art thou, love, a flower in my sight,
Whose beauty blooms, but cannot long endure,
I dread the coming of the chilling night,
When this dear vision shall no more be sure.
Yet while it lasts, I'll cherish every hour,
And hoard its scent within my heart's own bower.

###25###
When weary thoughts do trouble my repose,
And slumber flees, a most unwelcome guest,
My mind doth wander where the darkness grows,
And seeks in vain a moment's peaceful rest.
The phantoms of the day do rise anew,
With whispered doubts and fears that chill my soul,
And all the world appears in somber hue,
Beyond my strength, beyond my poor control.
But then, dear love, thy image doth appear,
A beacon bright within the Stygian shade,
And all my worries vanish, and I fear
The coming dawn, when thy dear light must fade.
So I do long for sleep, that I may dream
Of thee, my sun, my life, my guiding gleam.

###26###
The gentle rain doth fall upon the earth,
And bids the seeds to stir and break their shell,
A promise whispered of a new rebirth,
Where life and beauty once again shall dwell.
The thirsty ground doth drink with eager thirst,
And nourishment within its bosom finds,
As verdant shoots do bravely travel first,
To greet the sun, and leave their earthly binds.
So too my heart, though parched by lonely years,
Doth yearn for comfort, and a gentle touch,
To quench the drought of sorrow and of tears,
And bloom anew, with joy it craves so much.
O let thy love, like rain from heavens sent,
Upon my barren soul be kindly spent.

###27###
The fleeting hours, like birds upon the wing,
Do pass so swiftly, ere we can espy
The joys they bring, the songs they sweetly sing,
And then are gone, beneath a distant sky.
I strive to hold them, with a desperate hand,
To keep their brightness, and their merry sound,
But they escape, and leave me on the sand,
Where empty echoes can alone be found.
My dearest love, thy presence makes them stay,
And gives to time a meaning and a grace,
Each moment spent with thee doth chase away
The shadow of this ever-fleeting pace.
So let us seize the day, and hold it fast,
And make our present joys forever last.

###28###
The storm doth rage, with thunder's awful sound,
And lightning's flash doth pierce the inky night,
The wind doth howl, and shake the solid ground,
And fills the soul with terror and with fright.
The ocean's roar, a tempest uncontrolled,
Doth threaten all, with fury and with might,
And stories dark of shipwrecks can be told,
Of souls engulfed, and lost to endless night.
Yet in my heart, a calm I can maintain,
When thy sweet image doth before me rise,
Thou art my haven, safe from wind and rain,
My guiding star, that shines in darkest skies.
So let the tempest beat with all its force,
My love for thee shall be my steady course.

###29###
When I behold thy face, so fair and bright,
A thousand virtues do before me gleam,
Thy gentle spirit, shining with delight,
Doth fill my waking thoughts and my sweet dream.
No artist's brush, though skilled and deft it be,
Can capture half the beauty of thy mien,
The subtle grace, the sweet sincerity,
That doth adorn thee, fairest of the queen.
Thy voice, a melody that fills the air,
Doth chase away all discord and all woe,
And in thy gaze, a love beyond compare,
Doth bid my own true loving spirit grow.
So I am bound, by chains of sweetest love,
To thee, my queen, sent from the heavens above.

###30###
The passing years, like rivers to the sea,
Do bear us onward, to an unknown shore,
And what we were, we shall no longer be,
As time doth change us, evermore.
The laughter of our youth, the dreams we chased,
Do fade like mist, when morning sun appears,
And wisdom dawns, though with a bitter taste,
Of lessons learned through unavailing tears.
Yet in our hearts, a treasure may reside,
A love that deepens, as the seasons turn,
A bond of souls, where truth doth long abide,
And memories bright, that ever shall burn.
So let us cherish, as the years depart,
The steadfast love that dwells within the heart.

###31###
The winter's frost, doth hold the earth in sway,
And nature sleeps, beneath a mantle white,
The trees stand bare, awaiting brighter day,
And all is still, in slumber's silent might.
No flower blooms, no bird doth dare to sing,
The world is hushed, and waits for spring's warm kiss,
And hope deferred, doth sorrow often bring,
A longing for the season's former bliss.
But in this cold, my love for thee doth burn,
A steady flame, that no chill can abate,
And to thy warmth, my grateful spirit turn,
And find a refuge from the frosty fate.
So let the winter hold its icy reign,
My love for thee shall evermore remain.

###32###
When fortune smiles, and all the world is fair,
And joys abundant do our senses greet,
We dance and sing, and cast aside all care,
And think our happiness is quite complete.
But clouds do gather, swift and unforeseen,
And storms do break, with unexpected force,
And what was bright, now turns to somber mien,
And happiness doth alter its discourse.
So let us not in fleeting pleasure trust,
Nor build our hopes on ground that will not hold,
But seek a love that riseth from the dust,
A steadfast truth, more precious than fine gold.
For earthly joys do vanish like the dew,
But true affection ever shall renew.

###33###
The busy bee, doth labor all the day,
And gathers honey from the fragrant bloom,
Then homeward flies, to store its precious prey,
And banish hunger from its humble room.
It toils and strives, with purpose in its soul,
To build its hive, and make its sweet store last,
And though the labor doth its strength control,
It knows its efforts shall not be in vain cast.
So must my heart, with diligent regard,
Collect the moments, that with thee I share,
Each precious glance, each word, however hard,
To build a hoard of love beyond compare.
For when thou art afar, or I must roam,
This wealth of love shall be my dearest home.

###34###
When absence doth thy cherished presence steal,
And miles between us do our hearts divide,
The bitter pangs of longing I do feel,
And in my soul, where sorrow doth reside.
The days are long, the nights are dark and deep,
And all the world seems barren and so bare,
I long to hold thee, and my vows to keep,
And banish thoughts of solitude and care.
But then I dream, and in that vision bright,
I see thee near, and feel thy loving touch,
And for a space, all is again alight,
And I forget that I do miss thee much.
So in my dreams, I find a sweet relief,
Until thou comest, to end all my grief.

###35###
The honest friend, a treasure true and rare,
More worth than gold, or jewels of shining hue,
Whose faithful heart doth banish all despair,
And proves a constant, steadfast, loving clue.
When fickle fortune turns her face away,
And flatterers flee, like shadows in the night,
The true friend stays, and helps thee greet the day,
With solace, strength, and ever-shining light.
So let us prize, these souls of noble mien,
Who offer comfort, and a listening ear,
Whose loyalty, doth ever intervene,
And banish doubt, and conquer every fear.
For in this world, where treachery doth reign,
A faithful friend doth ease our earthly pain.

###36###
The moon doth climb, the queen of silent night,
And casts her silver beams upon the deep,
She watches o'er the world with gentle light,
While weary mortals slumber in their sleep.
Her face doth change, from crescent to full orb,
A mystic dance, for all the world to see,
And in her glow, our troubled thoughts absorb,
A sense of peace, and sweet tranquility.
So too, my love, thy presence doth inspire,
A quiet calm within my restless breast,
Thou art the moon, that doth my soul attune higher,
And bringest me a much-desired rest.
So let us gaze, upon this beauty rare,
And find our peace, beyond all worldly care.

###37###
When sorrow's hand doth press upon my heart,
And heavy sighs do issue from my breast,
I feel as though my very soul doth part,
And can find no true solace, nor true rest.
The world doth seem a place of endless gloom,
Where shadows lengthen, and the light grows dim,
And I am left within this lonely room,
To contemplate a melancholy hymn.
But then thy voice, a balm upon my ear,
Doth whisper comfort, and dispel the shade,
And all my fears do vanish, and I hear
A promise of a joy that shall not fade.
So let thy words, like music, soft and low,
Dispel my grief, and bid my spirit glow.

###38###
The fickle mind, doth oft times change its course,
And what it loved, it soon doth cast aside,
Like shifting sands, without a steady force,
Where fickle passions do forever ride.
One moment keen, to follow a new whim,
The next indifferent, to what it did pursue,
Its purpose lost, its vision growing dim,
And promises are broken, fresh and new.
So let me swear, by all that I hold dear,
My love for thee shall never waver, nor decay,
Through sun and storm, through laughter and through tear,
My heart shall beat for thee, and thee alone, each day.
For in this world, where constancy is rare,
My love for thee, I pledge beyond compare.

###39###
When age doth steal the vigour of my prime,
And wrinkles mark the passage of the years,
And strength doth wane, beneath the weight of time,
And dim the vision, and bring forth the tears.
When all the bloom of youth hath fled from me,
And beauty fades, like flowers in the frost,
And feeble steps do mark my destiny,
And all my former glories have been lost.
Yet still my love, shall burn with steady fire,
And in thy eyes, I'll see a youth renewed,
Thou art the sun, that doth my soul inspire,
And chase away the shadows, dark and crude.
So let us grow old, together, hand in hand,
And love each other, till we leave this land.

###40###
The silent stars, that stud the velvet sky,
Do tell a tale of ages long since past,
Of ancient worlds, that long ago did die,
And in their ruin, shadows still are cast.
They gaze upon us, with their distant light,
Uncaring sentinels of endless space,
And witness all, the darkness and the bright,
The rise and fall of every earthly race.
But in their gaze, a promise I do find,
Of constancy, and purpose, true and deep,
And though the world may leave me far behind,
My love for thee, its solemn vow shall keep.
So let the stars bear witness to my plea,
My heart shall evermore belong to thee.

###41###
When first I saw thy visage, fair and bright,
My heart did leap as doth a startled deer,
And banished from my soul all former light,
Replacing it with hope, dispelling fear.
Thy voice, a melody to ravish sense,
Doth charm the air and make the Muses sigh,
A beauty past all mortal recompense,
That doth outshine the sun that rules the sky.
No painter's brush nor poet's cunning quill,
Can e'er portray the grace that in thee lies,
A wonder that doth all my senses fill,
And captivate my ever-watchful eyes.
So shall I love thee till my days are done,
My dearest treasure, my most cherished one.

###42###
The creeping hand of time, with patient tread,
Doth steal away the bloom from fairest cheek,
And leaves behind the marks of years long fled,
When youth's bright banner was too proud to speak.
Yet in thy soul, a spirit pure and bold,
Doth shine with radiance that will not fade,
A treasure richer than all earthly gold,
By Virtue's hand in purest essence made.
Let wrinkles furrow deep and hairs turn grey,
Thy inner beauty shall endure the test,
And chase the shadows of decay away,
A beacon burning in my beating breast.
So while I live, my love shall ever grow,
And in thy spirit, endless joys shall flow.

###43###
When Fortune smiles and all things do go well,
And worldly cares do seem a distant dream,
My spirit then doth in thy presence dwell,
And basks within thy warm and loving beam.
But when the clouds of sorrow gather near,
And tempests of misfortune rage and blow,
Thy constancy doth banish all my fear,
And plants a seed of comfort, soft and low.
For thou art to me as the steady star,
That guides the sailor through the darkest night,
A faithful haven, though the seas be far,
A source of solace, and a guiding light.
So let the world its shifting fortunes send,
In thee, my heart shall find its truest friend.

###44###
The summer's lease hath all too short a date,
And winter's frost doth bite with chilling keen,
So beauty's reign, though glorious and great,
Is but a fleeting and a transient scene.
The rose that blooms with crimson pride and grace,
Must yield its petals to the autumn wind,
And swiftly time doth alter every face,
Leaving but withered memory behind.
But in thy worth, a deeper truth I find,
A constant flame that shall not be subdued,
A gentle spirit, ever true and kind,
By no external change to be eschewed.
Thus though the outward show may fade and die,
Thy inner virtues shall for aye thereby.

###45###
What though the critics carp and envious tongues
Do whisper falsehoods in the public ear,
And tune their venomous and bitter songs,
To stir within my loving heart a fear?
Thy virtue stands a bulwark strong and tall,
Against the slings and arrows of their spite,
And though they strive to make my spirit fall,
Thy steadfast truth doth shine with purest light.
Let slander rage and malice do its worst,
My faith in thee shall never be betrayed,
A love like ours, by heaven's decree nursed,
Shall stand unvanquished, by no foe dismayed.
So let them speak, their idle words are vain,
My heart with thine shall evermore remain.

###46###
When absent from thy sweet and loving gaze,
The world appears a barren, joyless place,
And all my thoughts do wander in a maze,
Of longing for the beauty of thy face.
The sun itself doth seem to lose its fire,
The birds do sing a mournful, doleful tune,
And all my soul doth yearn with deep desire,
To be again beneath thy silver moon.
Each passing hour, a heavy burden seems,
Until thy presence doth my spirits raise,
And banish all my melancholic dreams,
To fill my heart with bright and happy days.
So let me not be parted long from thee,
My dearest love, my sole felicity.

###47###
The ancient gods, who watched o'er mortal kind,
Did fashion thee with artistry divine,
A perfect form, most wonderfully designed,
To make all earthly beauty fall behind.
Thy lips, like coral, from the ocean's bed,
Doth spill forth words of honeyed, sweet delight,
Thy eyes, like stars, that light the overhead,
Doth fill my waking thoughts and dreams at night.
No sculptor's hand could mould such symmetry,
No poet's rhyme could capture half thy grace,
A living marvel, for all eyes to see,
The very image of celestial space.
So when I look on thee, my soul doth rise,
And finds its heaven in thy radiant eyes.

###48###
When shall I cease to ponder on thy worth,
And cease to count the virtues thou dost hold?
When shall the weary earth, with barren girth,
Cease to be richer than the purest gold?
My mind is occupied with thoughts of thee,
My heart doth beat with rhythm to thy name,
And all my being longs eternally,
To feed upon the brightness of thy flame.
Though reason bids me turn my gaze aside,
And seek a solace in a lesser fire,
My deepest instincts cannot be denied,
And draw me ever to my heart's desire.
So let me love thee, as I'm meant to do,
My soul's true compass, ever guiding true.

###49###
The fleeting moments of our earthly stay,
Are like the shadows that the sun doth cast,
They lengthen, shorten, and then fade away,
And leave no lasting imprint from the past.
But in thy love, a monument I see,
That time itself shall never can deface,
A steadfast promise, made eternally,
To hold me close within its warm embrace.
Let kingdoms fall and empires turn to dust,
Let fortunes crumble and let heroes die,
My trust in thee shall be my sacred trust,
Beneath the ever-watching, azure sky.
So in this love, my lasting peace I find,
A bond unbreakable, for heart and mind.

###50###
When winter's chill doth hold the land in thrall,
And naked trees do shiver in the breeze,
My spirit then doth seem to lose its all,
And sinks into a deep and cold unease.
But then I think of thee, my sun, my spring,
And all the warmth that from thy presence flows,
And with that thought, my frozen heart doth sing,
And banish all the ice that round it grows.
Thy laughter rings, a bell of purest sound,
Thy kindness melts the frost within my breast,
And in thy gaze, true comfort can be found,
A haven where my weary soul finds rest.
So let the seasons turn, and tempests blow,
Thy love shall be the source from which I grow.

###51###
The fleeting beauty of a summer's day,
Doth pass too quickly for the eye to hold,
And all its glories swiftly fade away,
Leaving but memories, both new and old.
So too, the bloom that on thy cheek doth lie,
Though fair and fresh, must yield to time's decree,
And ere long, shall the years begin to fly,
And change the outward semblance of my 'thee'.
But in thy soul, a garden I perceive,
Where virtues blossom, ever fresh and bright,
And from that richness, I shall e'er receive,
A deeper beauty, filled with inner light.
So though the outward form may fade and wane,
Thy truest beauty shall for aye remain.

###52###
When absence doth my heavy spirit press,
And distance parts us, as the cruel sea,
I find no solace in my loneliness,
But long to have thy loving company.
The world seems dim, and colours lose their hue,
The sweetest music sounds a mournful strain,
And all my thoughts do turn, alas, to you,
And all the joys that I do now disdain.
But in my dreams, I see thy gentle face,
And hear the echo of thy whispered word,
And for a moment, find a sweet embrace,
A phantom solace, though it be unheard.
So send me tidings, lest my hope should cease,
And bring my troubled, longing heart some peace.

###53###
The moon doth wax and wane with constant change,
And tides do ebb and flow upon the shore,
But still my love for thee shall not estrange,
Nor alter from the purpose held before.
The stars may shift their places in the sky,
And constellations alter their design,
But in my heart, where truth doth ever lie,
Thy image stays, eternally divine.
Let fickle fortune turn its fickle wheel,
And let the world its shifting favour lend,
My steadfast heart its loyalty shall feel,
And on thy constancy, my soul depend.
So rest assured, my love shall never stray,
But burn with passion, through each passing day.

###54###
When sorrow's shadow doth my spirit blight,
And dark despair doth claim its heavy toll,
I turn to thee, my soul's unfailing light,
And find within thy warmth my spirit whole.
Thy gentle voice, a balm to woundéd ear,
Thy tender touch, a solace to my pain,
Doth banish every doubt and every fear,
And make my strength and courage rise again.
No greater comfort can this world bestow,
Than in thy presence, find my heart at ease,
And watch the seeds of hopeful joy to grow,
Beneath the shelter of thy loving trees.
So let me ever in thy care abide,
My truest comfort, my most faithful guide.

###55###
The fleeting rose, that greets the morning sun,
Doth shed its petals ere the day is done,
And beauty's reign, though glorious and won,
Is but a span, ere time hath overthrown.
But in thy soul, a deeper grace resides,
A constant bloom that time cannot subdue,
A virtue that forevermore abides,
And makes thy spirit ever fresh and new.
Let wrinkles come, and hair begin to grey,
Thy inner light shall shine with brighter gleam,
And chase the fading outward show away,
A perfect truth, a more enduring dream.
So in thy soul, my lasting love I find,
A treasure worthy of my heart and mind.

###56###
When cruel fate doth seek to tear apart,
The tender bonds that hold our souls so near,
And whispers doubts within my loyal heart,
To sow the seeds of jealousy and fear.
I look to thee, and see a truth so plain,
That mocks the falsehoods of their wicked art,
And in thy gaze, I feel my strength regain,
And banish all that would my peace impart.
Thy constancy, a shield against all strife,
Thy honest love, a beacon in the storm,
Shall guide me safely through this mortal life,
And keep my faithful, loving spirit warm.
So let them plot, their malice shall not thrive,
Our hearts united, shall for aye survive.

###57###
The busy bee, that hums from flower to flower,
Doth gather nectar, sweet and pure and fine,
So doth my love, in every passing hour,
Seek out the virtues that in thee do shine.
The gentle dove, that coos its soft lament,
Doth speak of constancy, both true and deep,
So shall my heart, on thy true love intent,
Its faithful promise ever, ever keep.
Though outward shows may often lead astray,
And promise fair, but inwardly be bare,
Thy inner worth doth guide me on my way,
A guiding star, beyond all worldly care.
So let me then, in thy true love reside,
My constant comfort, and my faithful guide.

###58###
When weary days do bring me low and faint,
And worldly cares do press upon my soul,
I find in thee a respite from all taint,
And feel thy love my wounded spirit whole.
Thy laughter, like a brook that softly flows,
Doth cleanse the dust from off my troubled mind,
And in thy gaze, a hopeful comfort grows,
A sanctuary, where true peace I find.
Let storms arise, and tempests round me blow,
Thy steadfast heart shall be my resting place,
And in its warmth, my love shall ever grow,
And find its solace in thy sweet embrace.
So let me not from thy dear side depart,
Thou art the anchor of my beating heart.

###59###
The fleeting shadow of a summer's cloud,
Doth pass across the sun, and then is gone,
So too, the praise of an unthinking crowd,
Doth fade as quickly as the early dawn.
But thy true worth, a treasure to behold,
Doth shine with radiance that will not dim,
A story that shall never be untold,
A perfect portrait, from the very brim.
Let others seek the fickle, passing fame,
That glitters bright, but soon doth lose its fire,
My heart doth burn with an enduring flame,
Fueled by the passion of my soul's desire.
So let my verse thy lasting praises sing,
To thee, my love, all joy and honour bring.

###60###
When night doth cloak the world in solemn shade,
And stars do gleam, like diamonds in the sky,
My thoughts to thee, my dearest, are conveyed,
And in that silence, I do softly sigh.
The moon doth cast a pale and gentle light,
Upon the world, that sleeps in peaceful rest,
But in my heart, a more resplendent sight,
Thy image burns, forever manifest.
Though slumber claims the senses of the day,
My waking thoughts are wholly filled with thee,
And chase all other fleeting dreams away,
To find my solace in thy memory.
So till the dawn doth break, and light appears,
My love for thee shall conquer all my fears.

###61###
The rose that blushes with a crimson hue,
Doth boast a beauty that shall quickly fade,
And all its glory, fresh and bright and new,
Must yield to time, by nature's hand betrayed.
But in thy soul, a beauty I do find,
That ages cannot touch, nor years impair,
A gentle spirit, ever true and kind,
Beyond the reach of worldly, vain compare.
Let outward shows decline and outward grace,
Be stolen by the creeping hand of time,
Thy inner worth shall hold its rightful place,
A lasting monument, in reason's rhyme.
So let me praise the soul that shines so bright,
My dearest love, my everlasting light.

###62###
When weary from the world's incessant strife,
And all its clamour doth my spirit strain,
I turn to thee, the solace of my life,
And find within thy presence peace again.
Thy gentle voice, a balm to woundéd ear,
Thy loving gaze, a light to guide my way,
Doth banish all the shadows of my fear,
And make my troubled heart feel bright as day.
No earthly comfort can with thine compare,
No sweeter balm can wounded spirit find,
Than in thy love, to cast aside all care,
And leave the vexing worries far behind.
So let me ever in thy arms abide,
My truest comfort, and my faithful guide.

###61###
When Fortune smiles with her most radiant eye,
And paints the world with colours bright and bold,
Then doth my soul with joyful spirit fly,
And all my woes to pleasing tales are sold.
But when the clouds do gather, dark and deep,
And chilling winds do whisper tales of dread,
My heart with heavy sorrow starts to weep,
And joy from out my troubled breast hath fled.
Yet know I well this truth, though ill betide,
That seasons turn, and sun doth follow rain,
And though the deepest shadows may preside,
A brighter dawn will banish all my pain.
So let me learn to bear both sun and storm,
And find in all God’s will a truer form.

###62###
My love, whose beauty doth outshine the morn,
And doth eclipse the fairest stars above,
Hath by her grace a new existence born,
And filled my soul with everlasting love.
Her eyes, like jewels, do sparkle and do gleam,
Her voice, a melody both sweet and clear,
She is the substance of my waking dream,
And holds my heart, dispelling every fear.
No painter’s brush, nor poet’s finest quill,
Can e’er portray the wonders of her face,
My ardent passion shall her name fulfill,
And in her love I find my blessed place.
So let the world in adoration gaze,
Upon her worth, through all my earthly days.

###63###
The passing years, with their relentless pace,
Do steal the bloom from beauty’s tender cheek,
And furrow lines upon a once fair face,
Where youthful joys no longer seem to speak.
The silken hair, that once did shine like gold,
Now turns to grey, a silver, thin array,
And strength of limb, that was in youth so bold,
Begins to falter, fading day by day.
Yet in my mind, her image shall remain,
Untouched by time, forever fresh and bright,
A sacred shrine, where memory doth reign,
And fills my soul with pure and sweet delight.
So though the world may crumble and decay,
Her memory shall live beyond the day.

###64###
When I behold the fleeting hours fly,
And mark how swift our precious moments pass,
I fear lest love itself should fade and die,
Like shadows lost upon a looking-glass.
The bloom of youth, so vibrant and so fair,
Doth wither ere we scarce have time to know,
And all our hopes, that soar upon the air,
May to the dust of oblivion go.
But this I swear, by all the heavens above,
My love for thee shall never know an end,
It shall endure, a steadfast, burning love,
On which my heart shall evermore depend.
Though time may rob thee of thy outward grace,
My soul shall ever hold thee in thy place.

###65###
The weary soul, oppressed by worldly strife,
Doth seek a balm to ease its aching pain,
And longs for respite from this mortal life,
Where trials vex and sorrows ever reign.
The weight of care, the sting of sharp regret,
Do press upon the spirit, day by day,
And leave the heart with naught but anguish met,
As hope’s faint embers softly fade away.
Yet in the stillness of a quiet hour,
When thoughts like gentle streams begin to flow,
I find within a hidden, gentle power,
That bids the seeds of inner peace to grow.
So let me learn to find this inner light,
And banish darkness with its radiant might.

###66###
My dearest friend, whose counsel wise and true,
Hath guided me through paths of doubt and fear,
I find my spirit’s strength renewed in you,
And feel your presence ever, ever near.
When shadows fall and tempests rage around,
Your steady hand doth offer firm support,
And in your words, a solace can be found,
That stills the storm and brings my soul good sport.
Though fortunes shift and fickle chance may frown,
Your steadfast heart remains a constant star,
A guiding beacon in this worldly town,
That shines for me, no matter where we are.
So let us walk, through sunshine and through rain,
And share each joy and banish every pain.

###67###
When absence doth the lover’s heart torment,
And miles do stretch between two souls so dear,
Each passing moment seems a moment spent,
In wishing thou wert with me, ever near.
The sun shines not with half its former gleam,
The birds sing not their joyous, sweet refrain,
The world is but a melancholy dream,
And all is dulled by sorrow and by pain.
Yet in my thoughts, thy presence I do keep,
A vivid image, cherished and so bright,
And in my dreams, thy promises I reap,
And hold thee close throughout the lonely night.
So though we be apart, my love remains,
And conquers distance with its hopeful chains.

###68###
The painted face, that masks a bitter heart,
Doth speak of falsehoods, cleverly concealed,
And plays a false and disingenuous part,
Whose hidden malice shall at length be revealed.
The honeyed word, that drips with feigned delight,
May hide a serpent’s venom in its sting,
And outward show, though fair unto the sight,
May prove the source of deepest suffering.
So let me look beyond the outward guise,
And judge the spirit by its inward truth,
For honest souls are rarer than the prize,
That fortune grants to folly and to youth.
Let virtue be the guide of all my days,
And flee from those who tread deceitful ways.

###69###
When Phoebus doth his golden chariot drive,
Across the heavens, with a burning gaze,
My heart doth leap, and feels itself alive,
And basks within his bright and warming rays.
The earth awakes, with colours fresh and new,
The birds do carol, in the leafy trees,
And all the world, bathed in a glorious hue,
Doth seem to dance upon the summer breeze.
Yet know I well, this beauty will not last,
The season’s change, the frost will soon appear,
And all the summer’s glory shall be cast,
Into the tomb of winter, cold and drear.
So let me cherish every sunlit hour,
Before it yields to winter’s chilling power.

###70###
The fleeting moment, like a passing dream,
Doth vanish ere we scarce have time to hold,
And all our earthly glories seem,
But transient tales, too quickly to be told.
The strongest oak, that stands against the gale,
Must yield at last, to time’s unceasing might,
And fairest face, though it doth brightly hail,
Shall fade away, and vanish from the light.
So let us then embrace each precious day,
And fill our lives with deeds of worth and grace,
And leave behind a bright and lasting way,
That time itself shall never quite erase.
For in good works, a spirit doth reside,
That shall through all eternity abide.

###71###
When envious tongues do wag with wicked lies,
And seek to wound the innocent and pure,
My spirit rises, strong and with surprise,
And knows the truth shall evermore endure.
The poisoned word, that flies on airy wings,
May cause a wound, though shallow it may seem,
But righteous hearts, to whom true virtue clings,
Shall rise above the calumny and dream.
Let not their malice cause thee to despair,
Nor let their slanders dim thy inner light,
For justice waits, and time will make it clear,
And bring the truth into its proper sight.
So stand thou firm, with honour as thy shield,
And let thy virtues be in truth revealed.

###72###
My muse, that doth inspire my humble rhyme,
And lends a grace to words that else were plain,
Doth banish dullness and the weary time,
And makes my spirit soar and sing again.
When inspiration fails, and thoughts grow cold,
And barren lies the muse’s fertile ground,
No worthy verse can ever then unfold,
And all my efforts are of little sound.
But when thou deignest to descend to me,
And fill my mind with visions bright and bold,
My ready pen doth dance with eager glee,
And tales of love and beauty are unrolled.
So grant me, muse, thy everlasting aid,
And let my verse in timeless beauty be arrayed.

###73###
The heavy chains of duty, stern and fast,
Do bind the spirit, till it longs for flight,
And dreams of freedom, that shall ever last,
Beyond the reach of sorrow and of night.
The endless toil, the burdens that we bear,
Do press us down, with unrelenting weight,
And fill the heart with weariness and care,
And seal us to a predetermined fate.
Yet in the stillness, when the day is done,
And weary limbs may find a moment’s rest,
A secret joy, by freedom’s light is won,
And lifts the soul, and makes the spirit blest.
So let me strive, for that sweet liberty,
That sets the captive soul forever free.

###74###
When tempest’s rage doth shake the fragile bark,
And waves do crash with fury on the deck,
The sailor’s heart, cast into anxious dark,
Doth fear that life itself will be a wreck.
But when the clouds do part, and sun appears,
And gentle breezes whisper on the main,
The trembling soul dispels its former fears,
And finds a peace that banishes all pain.
So in our lives, though storms may often blow,
And trials test our courage and our might,
Let us not yield, but let our spirits grow,
And trust that dawn will follow darkest night.
For through the strife, our strength is truly found,
And on a shore of peace, we shall be bound.

###75###
The fleeting breath, that doth our lives sustain,
Is but a moment, precious and so rare,
And all our striving, all our earthly gain,
Shall vanish like a whisper on the air.
The monuments we raise, however grand,
Shall crumble down, by time’s relentless hand,
And all our power, scattered like the sand,
Shall leave no trace upon this changing land.
So let us then not hoard our earthly store,
Nor cling to pride, that fosters vanity,
But seek a treasure, that shall last evermore,
A soul enriched by truth and charity.
For in these deeds, a lasting worth we find,
That leaves a glory for all humankind.

###76###
When love’s sweet pangs do pierce the tender heart,
And leave it yearning, in a sweet unrest,
Each moment spent apart doth play a part,
In making passion burn within the breast.
The absent face, imprinted on the mind,
Doth conjure visions, tender and so fair,
And in each thought, true solace I do find,
And breathe thy name upon the silent air.
Though absence may inflict a sharpest sting,
It serves to prove the depth of love’s embrace,
And makes the joy of meeting doubly sing,
When at the last, we stand in each other’s grace.
So let this absence, though it pains me sore,
But bind our loving hearts for evermore.

###77###
The silent night, with stars that softly gleam,
Doth hold a peace, that day can seldom bring,
And in its hush, I often fondly dream,
Of joys that future hours may yet uncling.
The weary world, with all its bustling sound,
Doth find a quiet, when the moon is high,
And in that stillness, true content is found,
Beneath the vastness of the midnight sky.
So let me learn, from slumber’s gentle art,
To find repose, when worldly cares do cease,
And in the quiet of my own deep heart,
Discover solace, and a lasting peace.
For in the night, when shadows softly creep,
The soul finds rest, and heavenly secrets keep.

###78###
The sting of envy, like a viper’s bite,
Doth poison souls, and warp the clearest sight,
And fills the heart with darkness and with spite,
And steals away the spirit’s purest light.
The covetous eye, that ever seeks to gain,
More than is given, by a generous hand,
Doth find its pleasure mingled with its pain,
And treads a path that few can understand.
So let me learn to be content with mine,
And find my joy in what the fates bestow,
For grasping hands do seldom truly shine,
And discontent breeds naught but bitter woe.
Let gratitude’s sweet song forever play,
And drive all envious thoughts far, far away.

###79###
When youth’s bright vigour doth begin to fade,
And strength departs, with every passing year,
The proudest heart, that once was unafraid,
Doth learn to bow, to time’s relentless fear.
The summer’s bloom, that once did brightly gleam,
Now withers fast, beneath the autumn’s chill,
And all our hopes, that soared as in a dream,
Must yield to age, and time’s insistent will.
But though the body weakens and grows frail,
The spirit’s fire may still brightly burn,
And in the wisdom that shall then prevail,
New strength and understanding we may learn.
So let me age, with grace and quiet pride,
And let true wisdom be my constant guide.

###80###
The simple flower, that grows by humble stream,
Doth hold a beauty, that doth far outshine,
The gaudy show, that doth in courts oft teem,
With hollow pomp, and purpose not divine.
No jewelled crown, nor robe of silken thread,
Can match the grace of nature’s sweet design,
Where honest truth, and beauty are instead,
Displayed for all, with purpose so benign.
So let me shun the gilded, false display,
And seek the truths that nature doth impart,
For in her simple wonders, I do lay,
The dearest treasures of my yearning heart.
And learn that virtue, in its humble guise,
Is fairer far than all the world’s disguise.

###81###
When fickle Fortune turns her wheel around,
And casts me down from heights I knew before,
No bitter tears upon the earth are found,
But quiet strength, that bids me to explore.
The loss of wealth, the fall from high estate,
May wound the pride, and cause the spirit pain,
But inward riches, that I truly rate,
Shall lift me up, and make me whole again.
For honour’s worth, and kindness of the soul,
No earthly power can ever take away,
They are a balm, that makes the spirit whole,
And guide my steps, along a brighter way.
So let me learn, from trials that betide,
That truest wealth doth evermore abide.
